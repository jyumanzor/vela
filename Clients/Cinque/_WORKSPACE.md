## Photo workflow

1. Raw scans / exports land in `assets/<country>/`.
2. Curated selections copied into `site/public/photos/<country>/` for use on the site.
3. `assets/photo-manifest.json` tracks what's in the archive.
4. `site/public/photos/` is what Vercel serves; `assets/` is what Jenn pulls from.

Do not commit `assets/` into the `site/` repo. If the archive grows past a few hundred MB or needs to be shared, move to object storage (Vercel Blob / R2) or a separate archive repo.

## Naming / versioning

- Repo branches: `main` for the deployed line. Feature branches off main (`feat/...`, `fix/...`, `content/...`).
- Photos keep original filenames from the scan source (e.g. `R1-06254-0004.JPG`, `000047020007.jpg`).

## Cleanup rules

- `node_modules/` is regenerated by `npm install`. Delete freely if space matters.
- `.next/` and `tsconfig.tsbuildinfo` — regenerated on build. Delete freely.
- `.vercel/` — machine-local Vercel link. Do not commit. Regenerated by `vercel link`.
- Never delete `assets/` without confirming with Jenn. It is the raw archive.
- Do not remove the iCloud mirror. It is the restore fallback.
